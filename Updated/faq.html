Common Questions

Can I get infected by drinking contaminated water?

No. You can only be infected when contaminated water goes up your nose. Swallowing the amoeba is not harmful as stomach acid kills it.

How common are infections from Naegleria fowleri?

Infections are extremely rare. In the United States, for example, there are only a handful of cases reported each year. However, the infection is so deadly that prevention is very important.

Can I get infected in a properly maintained swimming pool?

No. Standard levels of chlorine and disinfection in swimming pools are effective at killing Naegleria fowleri.

Is Naegleria fowleri found in salt water like the ocean?

No, it is a freshwater amoeba and cannot survive in salt water.

Can the infection spread from person to person?

No, Primary Amebic Meningoencephalitis (PAM) is not contagious.

When is the risk of infection the highest?

The risk is highest during the summer months when air and water temperatures are high, typically from July through September.
