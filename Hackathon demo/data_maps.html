From Manual Input to a Global System

Our manual risk checker demonstrates a proof-of-concept. The true power lies in automating it with real-time data from NASA's Earth-observing satellites to create dynamic risk maps.

1. Collect Satellite Data

Satellites like Landsat and MODIS measure Land Surface Temperature, which helps estimate water temperature. NASA's GPM mission tracks precipitation.

2. Analyze Risk Factors

An algorithm processes this data, searching for high-risk factors: sustained high water temperatures, low water levels, and lack of recent, cooling rainfall.

3. Generate Predictive Maps

The system visualizes this analysis on a map, flagging water bodies with color-coded risk levels, providing an easy-to-understand public warning system.
