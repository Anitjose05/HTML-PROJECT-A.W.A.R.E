A Spreading Danger in a Warmer World

*Naegleria fowleri* is a thermophilic (heat-loving) organism. As global temperatures rise due to climate change, its natural habitat is expanding, posing new risks to public health in previously unaffected regions.

Expanding Geographic Range

Historically, infections were limited to southern regions. However, rising average temperatures in lakes and rivers are allowing the amoeba to survive and establish populations further north.

Longer 'Amoeba Seasons'

Warmer temperatures are extending the duration of summer and shortening winters. This creates a longer recreational water season, increasing the time people are exposed to water when it is warm enough for the amoeba to thrive.

NASA's Crucial Monitoring Role

Monitoring these subtle but critical environmental shifts is a key mission for NASA. Satellites like Landsat provide thermal data to track water temperature changes, which is vital for updating our risk models.
