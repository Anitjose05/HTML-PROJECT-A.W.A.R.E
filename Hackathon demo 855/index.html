An Overview of Meningitis

Meningitis is an inflammation of the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It can arise from various sources, each with different levels of severity.

Bacterial Meningitis

A serious medical emergency that can be fatal. It is often preventable with vaccines and requires immediate treatment.

Viral Meningitis

The most common type. While serious, it is rarely fatal in people with healthy immune systems and often resolves on its own.

Fungal Meningitis

This type is rare and typically only occurs in people with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV or cancer.
